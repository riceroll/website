Jianzhe Gu

Jianzhe [dʒendʒər] is a 4th-year Ph.D. in the Human-Computer Interaction Institute @Carnegie Mellon University, advised by Lining Yao. His research interest focuses on computational design, digital fabrication, human-computer interaction and computer graphics. Blending digits and materials, he devotes himself to exploring the future form of shape-changing interfaces that can be computationally designed, evolved, and generated. His research work was published at premier conferences of HCI, including CHI and UIST. Prior to joining the CMU, Jianzhe earned his bachelor’s degree in Electrical and Computer Engineering from Shanghai Jiao Tong University, China.
